# Explainable AI and AISTATS 2021

While a lot is going around in the Explainable AI community, this year 4 papers were published at AISTATS 2021 related to this field.

1. Have We Learned to Explain?: How Interpretability Methods Can Learn to Encode Predictions in their Interpretations by Neil Jethani, Mukund Sudarshan, Yindalon Aphinyanaphongs, Rajesh Ranganath

> This work proposes a selector and evaluator method namely REAL-X and EVAL-X in order to reduce any loss of fidelity in the interepretation of models. They show the emperical proof of concept of radiology dataset.

2. Improving KernelSHAP: Practical Shapley Value Estimation Using Linear Regression by Ian Covert, Su-In Lee

> This work proposes approxmation of shap values by exploiting the linear regression characterization of the shap values calculation objective. 

3. Shapley Flow: A Graph-based Approach to Interpreting Model Predictions by Jiaxuan Wang, Jenna Wiens, Scott Lundberg
