## About Me

I am currently a research engineer at ATILF, Université de Lorraine, France, working with <a href="https://perso.atilf.fr/mconstant/">Mathieu Constant</a> on the <a href="https://www.starfle.fr/en/mesmerize/">STAR-FLÉ</a> project, where I am exploring interpretability in the context of multi-word expressions and large language models.  

I completed my Phd at Université de Lorraine, Nancy, France, last December, during which my research involved studying the behavior of medical language models and their interaction with medical text data  under the supervision of <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/marianneclausel/home?authuser=0">Marianne Clausel</a>, <a href="https://perso.atilf.fr/mconstant/">Mathieu Constant</a> and <a href="https://xavieratcern.github.io/index.html">Xavier Coubez</a>.
More concretely, I was interested in the interaction between <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2024.bionlp-1.16.pdf">model performance</a>, <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.269.pdf/">data perception variability</a>, and  <a href="https://actionable-interpretability.github.io/posters/159_simplicity_is_not_as_simple_as_you_think%20-%20Aman%20Sinha.pdf">interpretability</a>.

And more recently, I’ve developed a fondness for hallucinations in large language models. \
