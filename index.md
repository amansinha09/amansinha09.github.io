## About Me

I am an incoming Postdoctoral Researcher at the <a href="https://ds4dh.unige.ch/">DS4DH Group</a>, Faculty of Medicine, Université de Genève, Switzerland. I will be working with <a href="https://www.unige.ch/medecine/radio/en/research-groups/1035teodoro/membres-du-groupe/douglas-teodoro">Prof. Douglas Teodoro</a> and <a href="https://www.unige.ch/medecine/en/faculty/decanat/delegue-es-au-decanat/prof-caroline-samer">Prof. Caroline Samer</a> on the SNSF-funded project <a href="https://data.snf.ch/grants/grant/10005385">CTRAI2</a> about AI-based Risk Assessment for Clinical Trials on Medicinal Products.  

I completed my Phd at Université de Lorraine, Nancy, France, last December, during which my research involved studying the behavior of medical language models and their interaction with medical text data  under the supervision of <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/marianneclausel/home?authuser=0">Prof. Marianne Clausel</a>, <a href="https://perso.atilf.fr/mconstant/">Prof. Mathieu Constant</a> and <a href="https://xavieratcern.github.io/index.html">Dr. Xavier Coubez</a>.
More concretely, I was interested in the interaction between <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2024.bionlp-1.16.pdf">model performance</a>, <a href="https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.269.pdf/">data perception variability</a>, and  <a href="https://actionable-interpretability.github.io/posters/159_simplicity_is_not_as_simple_as_you_think%20-%20Aman%20Sinha.pdf">interpretability</a>. Read more about my doctoral thesis, <a href="https://theses.hal.science/tel-05576224/document">here</a>.

And more recently, I’ve developed a fondness for hallucinations in large language models. \
